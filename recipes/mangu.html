Dominican Mangu

Learn today how to make traditional Mangu

What is Mangu?

Mangú is a popular Dominican side dish that’s made with mashed plantains and can be served with meat, eggs, and cheese. See how to prepare mangú here.

Creamy, rich, and delicious, mangu is one of my favorite plantain-based recipes. Not only is it very simple to make, it’s also very versatile as you can make it the main dish for either breakfast, lunch, or dinner. Served with quick pickled red onions, this is an easy and nutritious recipe full of potassium, vitamin c, magnesium, and many other benefits that make for a wonderful meal.

Ingredients
  • Green Plantains
  • Butter
  • Salt
  • Water

Directions

  1. Peel the plantains and cut lengthwise, then divide each half into two. Check out my post on How to Peel Plantains quickly and easily.
  2. Add the plantains to a pot and boil over a medium high heat in enough water to cover them and salt until they are very tender.
  3. Remove the plantains from the water and mash them right away with a fork until they are very smooth and there are few to no lumps. Mix in butter, and water. Keep mashing and mixing until smooth.
